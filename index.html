Traditional platforms test coding.
1Code tests how you think.

You solve realistic software bugs using AI, submit your AI conversation, and get rated on your debugging process — not your typing speed.

Real bugs, not puzzles

Every challenge is a realistic project with a real symptom — the kind you'd actually hit in production, not an algorithm riddle.

Scored on process, not speed

// the workflow

From symptom to score, in one AI-assisted sprint

Twelve steps, one submission. Scroll to walk through it.

01

Login / Register

Create your account and pick up where you left off — your rating persists per domain.

02

Choose your debugging mission

Browse challenges by domain — Auth, Database, Performance, Security — and pick your difficulty.

03

Read the scenario

Just the symptom. No code, no stack trace — the same way a real bug report reaches you.

04

Write initial hypotheses

List what you'd suspect first. This is where domain experience shows before you've seen a single line.

05

Prioritize your hypotheses

Rank them by likelihood — the order you'd actually investigate in, under time pressure.

06

Download challenge.zip

A real local project drops onto your machine.

buggy_project/ README.md debug_log.txt
07

Debug locally, with any AI tool

ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Cursor, Copilot — your call. We care about how you use it, not which one.

ChatGPTGeminiClaudeCursorCopilot
08

Fill debug_log.txt

Paste every prompt you sent and every response you got back — the full trail, not just the winning one.

09

Fix the bug

Apply the fix locally until the symptom is actually gone.

10

Create submission.zip

Package your work for grading.

fixed_project.zip debug_log.txt
11

Upload submission

One shot, one upload. That constraint is intentional — it's what makes the rating mean something.

12

AI evaluation → Score report → Leaderboard

Your process gets graded across four dimensions, and your domain rating updates.
